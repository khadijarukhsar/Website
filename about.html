About Me

People generally like talking about themselves.

For years, I've been trying to communicate with people the way I'm supposed to, but whenever someone asks me about myself, I realize how little I know. I know my name—it's Khadija. I know I'm 21 years old. But then what? What else is there to say?

Perhaps this is where the journey begins.

I am a miracle in my own way; my birth was unexpected. I am hope for those who suffer around me. I try to be a helping hand whenever someone needs one. I'm a good listener, so people often share their lives with me—their struggles, their joys, their achievements, and their fears. I'm a loyal friend, though sometimes I distance myself more than I'd like to admit.

At times, I'm chaos. At times, it feels as though nothing can be changed. Yet I remain optimistic. No matter how dark the night becomes, I believe there will always be a dawn.

I am deeply curious about the world. I want to understand not only what happens around me, but why it happens. Why do people do the things they do? What shapes their choices, beliefs, and desires? These questions have followed me for as long as I can remember.

I love books. They have been some of my closest companions. I am drawn to philosophy, psychology, and literature because they help me explore the mysteries of human nature. I'm endlessly fascinated by how human beings can be so complex and yet so simple at the same time.

Above all, I am a seeker—of knowledge, of meaning, and of guidance. I strive to become the person God would be pleased with, while continuing to learn, grow, and understand both myself and others.

And in the end, perhaps this isn't really about me at all.

I've never liked defining myself. To define is to limit. Human beings are always changing, always becoming.
