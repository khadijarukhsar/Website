When Misery Becomes Trendy

I have heard and read hundreds of stories. Where the narrators claim that knowing too much, being too self-aware, has destroyed them. I disagree. At so many levels. And the fun part is one of those narratives used to be my own. I was suffering and trapped. Even before I knew what was happening. Knowing about it did not initiate it. It existed anyway. If knowing too much is making you overwhelmed, it just means you know very little. Or maybe you have skipped the important foundational parts. Maybe first learn how to handle your concept of self. Before exploring its hidden aspects. Maybe first learn what is the nature of self or knowing. Yes, it is the philosophical part. The one that is often seen as a waste of time. Claiming something has destroyed or trapped us, limits us from being free souls. That are ever-evolving. To someone who has been put in a box. I refuse to claim that my self is an idea or identity, that can be put inside a box. With a specific label on it. At least in this world. Yes, I get it. People form such beliefs to have a catharsis. To make a sense of their knowing and still suffering. And this is where the problem lies. Knowing about something does not mean that you have conquered it. You have just acknowledged its existence. Yes. A crucial step. But it is only a basic one. The start of the journey. So why expect the rewards and destination, when you have only just started the journey? I get it that we humans need some moments to mourn. And the journey is never a linear one. There are the moments when our emotions consume us completely. Any logic, any solution, loses its value. But I have seen people (and myself) just get stuck here. As if, it's the destination. Something to be romanticized. Sometimes, it is necessary to feel bad. So you can feel good to the optimal contrasting level. But to appreciate this point, this good, to make feeling bad worth feeling, you have to come out of the bad situation. You cannot passively wait for this feeling to go away, while having the belief that you are going to suffer anyway. Even if you try to know more. To explore more. Your life. Your mind. And your self. These three are waiting for you to become their active pilot. Autopilot sounds non-chalant, trendy and low-maintenance. But you can't deny even in this state that you are condemned to be free. You have will power. You are required to take an action. Actively. Consciously. With passion and compassion. To get out of these spirals. Or at least trying to.
