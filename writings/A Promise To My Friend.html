A Promise To My Friend

A Promise To My Friend (I hope I fulfilled it! ) Our minds are naturally more inclined to entertain negative emotions. And when life gives them an opportunity. It takes it wholeheartedly. Sadness, grief, yearning, nostalgia. These are all my old friends. But lately, I've befriended happiness too. Or at least I've tried. Sometimes I feel that I'm really happy. And then I look inside my mind. Only to see sadness sitting like a royalty. I don't bow and choose to ignore. It calls me blasphemous and drags me to the shore. Separating me from my new friend. My ocean of happiness. I meet him again. But the orders of separation always seem to be settled between us. I smile. I laugh. But I know something else is there. I try to ignore it. And suddenly. I can't feel the happiness anymore. I've learned it from an early age. That you can't choose to feel certain emotions. And not others. If you do, then you'll go numb. Not feeling one thing deeply can make you unreactive to other emotions too. It reminds me of this quote by Sylvia Plath, > "I don't know what it is like to not have deep emotions. Even when I feel nothing, I feel it completely." To feel deep is to live deep. And to live deep is to suffer deep. But here's an imbalance. To take negative emotions for home. And question the positive as the fake ones. Maybe I'm not truly happy and I'm just faking it. When such whispers occupy my mind, I resonate with sadness more. Yearning makes more sense. Grief feels more like home. The positive ones should be felt with the same intensity too. Like giving life to someone. Like making everything worth doing. Every moment, worth living. Every breath, worth experiencing. But it's not like that when experienced practically. My home has become my cage. And the worst thing is, I love it. The sky seems soothing too. But what if I go there and find out it's all just an illusion. That will bring sadness too. Sadness, my old friend. And if I have to return to my old friends. Then why bother leaving them in the first place. But here's the thing. I'm not content with these friends. No matter how old they are. No matter how fake someone new is being perceived. I'm always somehow lured by the idea of having their company. If returning home is my destiny, then those feet that are taking me away from it are mine too. If I've accepted that I'll certainly return. A flickering hope of experiencing this again has always surrounded me too. If this was all real. Why do I keep going away for this? Maybe it's not the only thing real. Yes. Recently, I've experienced it. Ignoring old friends can actually sometimes shift our moods. For someone beloved, I dared to get rid of my old friends. Not permanently. But just for a while. And I could see that I can do it. So maybe this is the matter: I'm not supposed to cut them off. Nor can I do that. But I'm not supposed to be with them all the time either. I can leave them. And visit them again. And if they try to visit me. While I'm away. I can say I'm busy with my new friend. And I'll come to you later. It sounds so simple. Yet I know it's not. But it can be done. My experience has told me. And while I finish this reflection. I can proudly say. I'm happy that I wrote it. Before letting my mind force it away. I'm happy with my new friend. And I can visit old ones later. Maybe with the passage of time, I'll stop visiting them. But I dare not say it aloud. Because I fear. That they'll become violent. So for now, I'll just let things be as they are. And try to accept them as they are. With my mild, patient and tender efforts.
