Entry 13
Would you still love me if I was a worm? (As a friend, of course.) I have loads of energy. In contrast to my body. Which body? I don't know. Maybe it is in the body. I can't really tell, if I am my body, or my mind. Practically, I am both. But I think my mind dominates me. For I live inside it. A cage. An embodiment that is metaphysical. But it gives me a sense of existence. That may feel abstract, but it's still there. It is not like being deep into thinking and forgetting everything else. It's like the only source of living. The only sensation of life. Can you imagine living a life that can be detected by your logical mind only? When feelings are faded. Emotions have lost their density. Floating weightless. Existing not existing. This is one extreme. And blended with it, is another. Sometimes, I believe, I am a worm inside my body's brain. A pink one. A cute one. The worm is mostly sleepy. And loves to sleep even if she's not. Tired, exhausted. Floating like ghosts and carrying on tasks. Sitting on a comfortable chair. With a blanket on her lower end. With buttons ahead of it. A red button. For all: Anger, Frustration, Annoyance, Irritability, Overstimulation. Everything is controlled by one. The worm presses it and this whole factory of organs starts responding. In ways that are extreme and intense. I highly doubt that it is effective. As its impact is often amplified. Next to it is a rusted lever that is turned on. It controls my shutdowns. It is stuck. Takes hours and hours to settle. Then there is a group of small black buttons. They all look alike. And are there for a show. The worm gets ready. Puts on her ribbon dress. And a dark burgundy lipstick. She closes her eyes and presses randomly. My eyes become a screen. The worm watches a show. Of creatures moving left and right. Being offensive or defensive. Either yelling or explaining. There's something distinct that is envious for the worm. Emotions that feel real. Not in its factory. But on fellow creatures. Another button is pressed. And now the windows are flooding. Hiding the view. The worm feels trapped. She is afraid of water. Although, she drinks it a lot. She tries consuming it. Because it attempts to consume her. It makes her body expand. But she'll sleep and become alright. The view changes with another button. There appear long black and white ropes. Looking horrifying. Again, blocking the view. The worm is convinced that sometimes it can feel another control center too. Maybe another worm lives inside the factory. Watching views. Changing them. It all becomes: Like a mind with an embodiment, and body that goes on its own, both coexist. There is a gap between the two. The gap is a living being or a lived experience. The worm is its lineage. Sometimes the windows capture a kitten, rolling a ball. And licking her feet. The windows are a slideshow of the weather too. Foggy darkness. Blinding sunlight. Scary storms. Cold rains. Even in these extremes there are some beautiful sights. An autumn tree soaked in rain. A cup of green tea. A cozy blanket. Or something to eat. And then there are tragic ones. A novel that is just started and not being read. The tragic events make the windows bidirectional. A person standing. Looking across the window hoping for an answer. The worm is stuck too. Maybe she has a rusty lever too. Becoming too lethargic to put her energy into anything. Rush of words running everywhere, the worm can't grab it to feed it to its listener. The listener stays hungry and the worm empty. Even yet filled with guilt and resentment. The emotional experiences of the worm are intense. She has very little capacity to consider others when she herself is experiencing something. The worm is still a baby. Even if it’s growing. And most importantly, the worm is a friend. Trying to be a good one. Questioning why anyone would stay with her. When she can't even move at their pace. She has a letter placed on her desk. She wrote it last night, when she couldn't sleep. She wanted to send it. But didn't. It will stay there for days. And will become dusty. The words will fade and no one will be able to read: Will you still love your friends if they were worms?
