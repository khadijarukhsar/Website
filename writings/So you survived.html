So you survived? (Did you?)

I usually try not to romanticize tragedies. but sometimes I admire a broken flower. its dried petals and fragrance of death. and feel inside my soul. how beautiful some tragedies can be at times. Who knew that just being what you're will be one of the hardest battles? I don't settle with blaming nature. it seems pointless. I can't be comfortable with the idea that everything that happened was a part of great plan. I'm the active player of my life. if things remain hidden and later in future they disclose, I find this idea seriously haunting. it makes me puppet. it makes nature alive. more alive than me. I'm a theif of life. I want the autonomy. I want to be responsible for what I do, what I achieve, what I lose. it's a big responsibility though. it's a foolish dream. these are the monologues that occupy my mind on a sunny day. that make me sink into the sadness. make me think sometimes that maybe it's okay to just pass this day sleeping. I can take a day off. can't I? the idea that there are multiple forces out there, that are responsible for your course of life. this idea has a child; there are multiple forces within me too. opposing, aligned, chaotic, dominant, silent. there are versions of myself that are more chaotic than others. and the thing is life keeps happening even if I don't want to continue with those versions. I don't want to live with uncertainty and life is full of uncertainties. denying uncertainty is denying life, and I'm here stuck to be or not to be. you know, when you don't know, who's controlling what, when you don't know, which version of you will decide your best, you just try to survive. it's not a solution. it's coping. often maladaptive and with many chaos. I'm terrified by a term called “self-sabotaging.” and the idea that such a thing exists, makes me terrified of my self too. terrified of my versions, that sometimes do make me feel alive. I've a long list of what ifs. i had a substack account before. I deleted it because that felt the easiest thing to sacrifice when blood was rushing in my vains so powerfully. to just do something. something that'll make survivor this version less chaotic. some kind of self-sabotaging. something that won't make me grief for a long time. the internet was slow that day, ironically. I thought nature had intervene. and that account wasn't gone. but here we are. days later I realized. that it is gone. you know what's the scary part? I was happy. I can start again. a beginning. however I like. the sense of control, it's so addictive. knowing that even if nothing feels in control, something is there that you can always try. and that's deleting the past, writing again. it may bring relief, but with a cost of endless questions. will this version survive? was it necessary? am I just going in circles and circles? trying to have a sense of control even if it means cutting a part off? that's the reason I'm afraid of the idea that even within myself, multiple forces exist. I can welcome them all, but welcoming the violent ones would automated mean giving up on the vulnerable ones. how can I do this to myself? how can I listen to two opposing parts of selves and decide which one has to go? when both makes sense, both feel like home, both are me. I know growth demands letting go. but here comes my second fear. self-sabotaging. I can't keep self-sabotaging in the name of letting go. that account was with my real name, and this is a made up. a part of the whole. just a version. this version costs my uncertain chaotic version. and the thing is. both are dear to me. I want to mourn what's gone. and this will complete the cycle of self-sabotaging. I want to see the dried petals. I want to smell the fragrance of death. I want to enjoy the aesthetics of being gone. to admire it's beauty. and this fear. this uncertainty. it won't make me settle for anything. and according to the theory, maybe this is the life. I'm the life. I'm gone. I'm witnessing myself gone. and I'm here. and I'm analyzing costs and benefits. and I'm questioning, did I survive? did I? if yes, when's the next act of letting go? or maybe self-sabotaging. I won't wait. not consciously at least. you know why? because it's haunting. so I'll follow that advice that seems so absurd and unpractical. I'll just stay mindful of the present. (it's a joke, but I'll try. many will call it denial, if not many, at least I do) I'll observe things and try not to sink in darkness. I'll take some deep breaths and see what chores I've to do now. so maybe, I did survive. this is absolutely not a mourning on deleting my previous account. it's much more. there's always much more.
