Reading Kafka was a mess

Reading Kafka was a mess, but healing in its own way. I've a theory about life: > "Each passing moment brings a new you, a new understanding of you and the world around you." When I read Kafka's words, I thought they fit me perfectly. Days passed, lessons learned; I read them again and they made more sense this time. Then, again, another journey, a new experience, a new interaction and a new relationship, everything became more meaningful. > "There are times, dearest, when I'm convinced, I'm unfit for any human relationship. " This is the quote that I experienced like this lately. The more I grow, the more I get involved with someone, the more I overcome my fear of connection, the more I see how unfit I'm. > "Once I read somewhere that once your start reflecting and meditating, you start finding more negativity within yourself. It's not because meditation and reflection bring negativity; it's because we start noticing ourselves more in both aspects (positive and negative)." I find my recent experience with the quote shared above aligned with this thought process. The more I try to connect, the more I realise the aspects in which I'm lacking. It's strange, isn't it? > "That the more we try to become someone, the more we realize how different we are." I was convinced that I was a safe space for someone. I thought I was available whenever they needed me; I listened and didn't judge. And then they gave me a mirror with a picture painted that seemed different from my perception of myself. I was distant and cold in their weak moments. I pushed them further whenever they tried to go away. They waited for me to pursue them, and I was distant, giving them space to sit with themselves. They wanted to sit with me and I was walking on narrow lanes, restraining, providing and dissolving into fog. I thought the fog would let them feel my abundance, without realising it could portray abandonment too. The darkness of the lanes was a safe space, created by mixing every colour of emotions and feelings. But it was a black hole too, with everything going inward and nothing coming out. I felt shallow and exhausted, like that fog was in my lungs, yes, that part of me, residing in me and making it difficult for me to breathe or speak. To tell or whisper that I care about you. To let you know that even when I was gone, I was with you.
