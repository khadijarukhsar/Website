Entry 06
Thoughts are Threads And what should I weave with them? To pass time with myself, I turn on the TV of my mind. I surf the channels to choose, what I want to watch and sit with. I see blackness on the screen giving the illusion of blankness. But when I look closely I find something there. The blackness is separated by blankness, like existence is contrasting abyss. I see spaces and boundaries, that are rigid and distinguishing. These are the threads of existence, of life and consciousness. Some threads are darker than others. And some of them are bright. And some bizarre; with bright shades of the dark. I want to touch them and feel them. I want to hold an end and walk along the thread, to see where it leads. Maybe it will lead to an old friend. And maybe another part of me is holding the string on the other side. I pull it further and find it stuck. It's tangled with the others. I shiver when I untangle them. I pull again and again and the thread keeps changing its color. From black to dark red, from fresh red to blue. I feel light headed while pulling the strings, like I'm undoing myself. Some burden seems off now, some heaviness gone. I spend the whole night doing the same. Only to realize in the morning that a part of me is gone. And another is vanishing too. And maybe devoluting into its threads. I become frightened and stop carrying on this quest. I stop it only to realise that the TV is half blank now. And beside it, is a pile of unwoven threads. I recognize them as nostalgia, and in fear of vanishing, I gather them with both arms. I hug them and try to lift them up. To pour them again, into the TV. To watch them again. To witness the chaos. But as a part of me. Not something separate from me. Not something abandoned. Not something gone.
