When Exhaustion Is The Only Way Through

When Exhaustion Is The Only Way Through (Everything Feels Like Walking On Eggshells) We humans have a very diverse range of feelings and emotions. Our emotional worlds shape our real world in ways that sometimes it is not exaggerated to say that they lead your lives. Lead the ways you choose to continue the journey of life. Either it’s about choosing a discipline for your higher education or choosing a relationship, our emotional world always plays an important role. These emotions are what help us make sense of what’s happening and sometimes expressing these emotions is what we need to breathe. In this entry, you’ll see me breathe, suffocate and breathe again. Walking on the road of life, I often stumble and fall. There are so many hurdles. So many choices. So many interests. So many hobbies. And then there are people. Their voices. Sometimes soothing. Sometimes unbearable. There are days when I can listen clearly. And then there are the opposite ones. I hear incomprehensible voices and try to see if someone is there talking to me. I always have to ensure it. Ensure that if a voice is criticizing me or offering some help, is it mine or someone else's. I'm not too proud to consider someone else's opinions or suggestions. I guess I'm not. I just feed on self autonomy. Independence. Yeah. This is my food. As the years are passing, as I'm growing, I'm realizing that I need it more. However, the resistance that was supposed to grow less with time, is increasing too. I have to ensure, if it's any part of me that's resisting. Or is it someone else. Someone existing in front of me in the present, or someone internalized. If it's me, there's another step for authentication. I have to be sure that this foreign concept of asking for help or even feedback is what I really want, or am I just influenced by someone. A foreign influence is a threat for me if I become conscious of it. It deeply targets my foundations leading me to cognitive dissonance. Turning letting go into a conflict between owning myself and growing. It leads to dramatic reactions. A friend of mine once told me that I react to these things like my world is ending. How to say that I truly feel that my world is ending. There's loads of destruction that sometimes leads to stability, if it doesn't remain chaotic forever. I encounter some thick smoky shadows luring me into the depths. The spirals of my thoughts turning darker and darker, causing me suffocation. I see ghosts trying to possess me. At first I didn't recognize them. However, they appeal to me in every stance. Ghosts of memories, people and my lost selves. They are stopping me from moving forward. Letting go. This isn't just in my mind. It's in my body as well. My racing heart and on-edge sensations won’t let me rest. I want to grow but the roots have become weakened with agonies. Uncertainties making familiarity a more lovely home. My feet feel drenched in the ground. Every step forward makes my inside scream. Blood. Death. Blood. Birth. Again death, again birth. The only way I can keep going is by crawling on my knees. Someone might call it survival, not living. My knees hurt carrying all my weight. It's embarrassing to stay alive sometimes. My legs are bruised with achievements and failures. Bruises, the shades of blue and purple. The shades of growing colder. Detached and numb. I look back and it’s foggy, blocking my view and filling my lungs. The past always has a peculiar torturing nature. Even nostalgia hurts. I look forward and see a hollow ground. The future is always a big red question mark. Full of uncertainties. The ground is separated by a transparent wall. I can touch it like a smart phone and scroll left and right to change what’s ahead of me. It's a barrier created by my imagination. That gives me solace when the real world is too much. Or too less. Sometimes the screen is bloomy and sometimes it's blank. I see shades of rainbow becoming white light and then vanishing in strong thunder strikes. Moving from the sweetness of the first summer rain in the desert to the coldness of Antarctica. My hands feel tired from this crawling. Numbed. Not there. Distant. Gone. I want to rest my head against this wall and sleep. A long sleep. With no dreams. Not death. Just a break. And when I sleep I demand from everyone, please don’t come across this wall otherwise I might puke. I don't want to listen to you. I don't want to listen to myself. I'm tired of listening. I'm over stimulated. Your voice may reach me so keep quiet for a while. I won’t be able to see you, and but your footprints, they will haunt me. Please get out of my territory. Please for my peace, let me sleep. Psychological Insights The struggle to understand if any idea or thought we are thinking about is actually ours or influenced by someone else can be explained by a need of self autonomy and introjection working against it. I would like to clarify something. There's nothing wrong with learning something from others; their ideas, thoughts, experiences etc. The thing I talked about in this essay is a phase that occurs before this learning phase. When everything is just chaotic and nothing makes sense. From this piece of writing, you can witness a glimpse of what role self autonomy and cognitive dissonation plays in the process of learning through interpersonal interactions. Primarily conceptualized by the philosopher Immanuel Kant, self autonomy is an individual's capacity to make decisions for himself. It's about making free, independent and rational decisions, prioritizing our personal values on external pressures. The point is, we don't live in a void. (Even if we feel like this.) Everyone around us has a force that sometimes clashes with our inner clock. To make the clock continue on its own pace, we're promoting our self autonomy. On the other hand, introjection is the process of taking different aspects of the world around us and internalizing it. The person takes in the values, attitudes, opinions, behaviors of those around him into his psyche. This term was introduced by a psychoanalyst named, Sándor Ferenczi. So this is the main conflict in this entry. The need to stay pure within ourselves and deny any contamination and the need to allow others' influence to come and pass, and sometimes stay. This is a quest of defining our identity by staying who we are or allowing external forces to mold us. A quest of understanding that the modifications the world offers are just pressures or opportunities to level up to the next step of growth. The exhaustion of being stuck by it. I used the term cognitive dissonation in this text. Let's look at this concept too and see its role here. Leon Festinger introduced the idea that there are times when what's in our minds (our beliefs) does not align with what we do (behavior). This is the core concept of cognitive dissonance. When our beliefs, values and attitudes differ from our behavior, we experience tension. Our identity is threatened. We say we are smart, we won't hurt ourselves intentionally, but we smoke and drink. We believe gossiping is bad but we meet our friends and have a long session of 'analyzing' others. Our mind is divided. It wants to know who we are. The one we're believing to be or the one we are practicing. In the context of this reflection, the tension is between believing that we don't have or want to have a rigid mind, but when someone suggests an idea or change of plan, instead of taking it as a new piece of information, we're stuck in letting it in and letting ourselves go. (It's just one of the many aspects of explaining this.) The final insight that I want to take away with you is that you don't have to figure out everything at once. First is the time to allow yourself to recognize this tension. To face that yes, you can actually do something you don't believe you will do. Or you can actually believe something that's not supported by your actions. It's a great mess. Let this mess be witnessed for a while. The solutions are many and our mind already knows them. For now, we have just one task: to be. To let it be. For a while. Let yourself witness your battles. We can act on the urge to get rid of it later. Every internal battle tells you something deep about yourself. Don't ignore these confessions of your mind. Let it go chaotic for a while. Let it. Author's Note: Hey fellows, I hope you found bits of yourself in these lines. Just read the last few lines again and let yourself be. Sometimes it's the ultimate kind of learning that we can do for ourselves. There's much more to come in the future. Stay tuned!
