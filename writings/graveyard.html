Entry 02
Once I went for a morning walk. In a graveyard. I saw a cloth buried inside the soil. There was a time when it had some presence in it. Maybe a life. A being was still there, motionless as a statue. It didn't laugh or cry or scream or feel shy. Finally being authentic, just existing as it wanted. Lifeless, but supposed to exist anyway. It decayed with time, layer by layer. It refused to be put together. Like it was supposed to be throughout its life. It happily gave up whatever came its way. To reach its core. The core had some radiance. Dispersed in the atmosphere. And this radiance had pulled me closer to it in the first place. It became a canvas. For something not spoken so much. But felt often. It displayed hollowness. Showcasing the emptiness of some folks. It got eaten by insects. Like a person exhausted by life affairs. It showed what the skin was hiding. That white pure matter. The purity got dissolved into the soil. And vanished. Did it make the soil pure or polluted? Did this authenticity do any good? Or was it denied as just another act of pretentiousness? Did the soil accept it as its extended self? Or did it abandon it with the consciousness that it had? God knows what it did to the soil. The ground might not tell me but I realized something. I traced its cracks with my fingers. And knew that I am soil too. I tried connecting to the ground. Placing both hands on it. I tried finding its heartbeat. But no, it was still. I placed them on my chest. And felt it there. Soo fast that I wondered if it would abandon both soils. And fly to the sky. Growing some little white wings. The answer was there. Always kept, but untouched. The purity of the body was being dissolved into my soil. I felt seen and acknowledged. Some peace wrapped around my roots. The body had purified this soil. While dissolving into nothingness. The daring nature. The audacity to be this free. I felt so jealous. For some, maybe nothing was there. But I had seen that colour of the absence. That was so vivid. That I wanted to capture it. Feeling jealous I took a sigh. Tried waving goodbye. But I chose otherwise. As it was against its purpose of expression. It had let go and so did I. While walking back towards the crowd of voices. I was happy that at some point, when my heart would rest. My body would become limitless. It will show what it hid throughout the life. A void. An absence. Some traces of existence. I don't know if somebody would witness it or not. But one thing for sure. It will certainly happen. The solitude that will lead to this form of self expression. That will be my last act of healing. I just always knew that solitude would heal me. And when I'll leave that white cloth behind. That will be my last betrayal. On the face of the soil. It will tell my story to those; who'll stop. And try to listen to what some voids have to say. Who can see the absence and colours together. And who are always fascinated. By the expressions of the soil.
