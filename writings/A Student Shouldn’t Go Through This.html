A Student Shouldn’t Go Through This

A Student Shouldn’t Go Through This (Even if it develops critical thinking skills.) Last month I read a quote by Virginia Woolf on substack. As a woman I have no country. As a woman I want no country. As a woman, my country is the whole world. It means, ideas or systems like nations and countries are the products of patriarchal systems and if we want to fix this system, we can't work with the institutions generated by it. Because these institutions are what provide the foundation for this system. We have to go beyond this. My sister once mentioned my face resembles Virginia Woolf's. And to be honest, it really does. But I'm sure a matching face doesn't lead to matching thoughts. But one thing can. And that's experience. Or more precisely, suffering. I was amazed to see how shared suffering can generate shared thinking patterns. I was sitting in my Clinical Psychology class and we were learning about its history. I was looking for any women's contribution to the field and guess what, not a single woman was mentioned in the given handouts. That day, it felt like I was reading about the ideas framed by men, in a world of men, to run the systems for men. My whole interest, gone. My passion, destroyed. I couldn't even bring myself to read. What they had to say. I read about a philosopher. Developed respect for his ideas. Then realize his views about women. How can I respect the words of someone who considers my gender my whole identity? Who considers this identity secondary from his own, something inferior, gone wrong. I read a writer's work. Begin appreciating it. Then realise he stole his wife's work. Discouraged her for her writing. Read her diaries and copied them. Without giving her any credit. Then I read about those scientists who got Nobel Prizes based on collective efforts with their spouses. But they never gave them any recognition. Or their part in the rewards. I can understand that efforts are being made for equality now. I can see rights are being given. I can see otherwise too. But there's something very disturbing going on with our education. I don't want to learn what certain men had to say about my courses. I want to learn ideas. The whole story. And the whole story comprises voices of all. Irrespective of gender. I'm concerned about my interests in education now. It seems meaningless, incomplete. I can learn on my own the unbiased histories. But I can't represent them in my exams because that's not what's asked. Funny how we despise something, and that's the only thing that we have to face. In order to get what we want. To get what we deserve. This was frustration of a student. Don't take it personally. But you know what. You can take it personally actually. It's up to you. Take it. And I'll go and read my notes now. That are incomplete. That don't evoke any interest in me. No matter how wonderful the ideas sound. To feel, it is what it is. And I have to be practical. Or maybe I'm being too sensitive. Or it's nothing new. Or maybe I should appreciate a little how critical this flawed system has made me. I can think about something. That seems like meaning nothing to others. Maybe that's the point of experiencing marginalization. It introduces you to new ways of thinking too. The ways that are ignored by the mainstream. Or by those who have accepted everything. Maybe I should thank God too. For letting me see that this thing exists. Or maybe I should ask everyone. Why did you make injustice so natural? That I've to think about. If my thinking makes it obligatory to show gratitude. Or to feel like the cursed one. Who has to see things that others find ordinary. Or nothing. And feel for the things. And for myself. And for others too. For living in incomplete worlds. No, I can't. It's a heavy task. I should do something light. Some assignment or quiz. And pay for being a part of this system. Cheers!
